🎯 Key Nutrition Tips

🩸 Iron for Growth

Give iron-rich foods (lentils, eggs, red meat) paired with vitamin C (orange, lemon) to improve absorption.

Iron is essential for oxygen transport and prevents anemia.

☀️ Vitamin D

10–20 minutes of morning sun daily and consume fortified milk. Supplement after physician consultation if deficient.

Supports bone health and calcium absorption.

🧠 Healthy Fats

Include nuts, seeds, fish oils, and avocado for omega-3 to support brain development.

Critical for cognitive function and neural development.

💧 Hydration

Drink 6-8 glasses of water daily. Limit sugary drinks and encourage water with meals.

Essential for all body functions and energy levels.

⚠️ Common Nutrition Concerns

For Picky Eaters

  • Serve small, frequent meals with familiar foods mixed with new options
  • Let family members help choose or prepare meals
  • Make meals colorful and fun-shaped
  • Avoid forcing; exposure takes 10-15 tries for acceptance
  • Model healthy eating yourself

For Weight Management

  • Focus on balanced meals, not restriction
  • Encourage physical activity as family fun, not punishment
  • Limit screen time during meals
  • Teach portion awareness without obsessing
  • Avoid labeling foods as "good" or "bad"

For Vegetarian/Vegan Individuals

  • Protein: Combine legumes with grains (rice + beans) for complete amino acids
  • Iron: Eat with vitamin C to improve plant-based iron absorption
  • B12: Fortified foods or supplements are essential
  • Calcium: Fortified plant milks, leafy greens, tofu
  • Omega-3: Flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Age-Specific Recommendations

👶 Toddlers (1-3 years)

  • High calorie needs: 1000-1400 cal/day
  • Whole milk for fat-soluble vitamins
  • Small, soft pieces to prevent choking
  • Introduce allergens early (peanuts, eggs)
  • 3 meals + 2-3 snacks daily

🧒 Age 4-8 years

  • 1200-1600 calories per day
  • Whole grain cereals and breads
  • Low-fat or fat-free dairy (2-3 cups)
  • Colorful vegetables daily
  • 3 meals + 1-2 snacks

👦 Teens (9-18 years)

  • 1600-2400 calories per day
  • Increased protein for growth
  • Iron-rich foods for menstruating girls
  • Calcium for bone density building
  • Regular physical activity (60+ mins)

🏥 Special Health Cases

For Anemia (Low Iron)

Foods to include: Red meat, chicken, eggs, lentils, spinach, fortified cereals

Boost absorption: Pair iron foods with vitamin C (citrus, tomatoes)

Avoid with iron meals: Tea, coffee, calcium supplements (take 2 hours apart)

For Constipation

Increase: Fiber (whole grains, fruits, vegetables), water intake, physical activity

Daily water: 6-8 glasses minimum

Foods to emphasize: Pear, prune, oatmeal, beans, leafy greens

For Allergies/Intolerances

Common allergens: Peanuts, tree nuts, milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, soy, wheat

Management: Strict avoidance, read labels, have emergency plan

Substitutions: Work with a registered dietitian for balanced nutrition

⚕️ When to Consult a Healthcare Professional

  • Poor growth or weight gain/loss
  • Persistent fatigue or letharness
  • Digestive issues lasting more than 2 weeks
  • Suspected food allergies
  • Special dietary needs (vegetarian, medical conditions)
  • Blood work showing nutrient deficiencies
