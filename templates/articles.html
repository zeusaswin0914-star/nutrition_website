Understanding BMI

Updated: December 2025

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It's calculated as: weight (kg) / height (m)²

Why BMI Matters

  • Helps identify if a person is at a healthy weight
  • Screens for potential weight-related health problems
  • Tracks growth or weight patterns over time
  • Guides nutrition and activity recommendations

BMI Categories

Healthy Weight: BMI between 5th and 85th percentile

Overweight: BMI between 85th and 95th percentile

Obese: BMI at or above 95th percentile

Underweight: BMI below 5th percentile

Healthy Habits

  • Eat balanced meals with vegetables, fruits, proteins, and grains
  • Limit sugary drinks and snacks
  • Get at least 60 minutes of physical activity daily
  • Sleep 8-10 hours per night

Nutrition and School Performance

Updated: December 2025

How Nutrition Affects Learning

Proper nutrition is crucial for brain development and academic success. Here's how:

Key Nutrients for Brain Health

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Support brain development and concentration

Find in: Fish, walnuts, flaxseeds

Antioxidants

Protect brain cells from damage

Find in: Berries, dark chocolate, leafy greens

B Vitamins

Improve memory and focus

Find in: Eggs, whole grains, legumes

Iron

Carries oxygen to the brain

Find in: Meat, beans, fortified cereals

Breakfast Matters

People who eat breakfast show better:

  • Memory and attention span
  • Problem-solving abilities
  • School attendance and behavior
  • Overall academic performance

Sample Healthy Breakfast

Whole grain toast + Peanut butter + Banana + Glass of milk
Provides: Carbs (energy), Protein (sustained focus), Potassium (brain function)

Building Healthy Eating Habits

Updated: December 2025

Start Early

Healthy eating habits formed early in life last a lifetime. Here are practical tips:

5 Key Strategies

1. Make Meals a Family Time

Eat together without distractions. It builds better habits and family bonds.

2. Eat the Rainbow

Different colored foods have different nutrients. Aim for variety at each meal.

3. Stay Hydrated

Drink water throughout the day. Limit sugary drinks and juices.

4. Involve Family in Cooking

People are more likely to eat foods they help prepare.

5. Limit Ultra-Processed Foods

Focus on whole foods: fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins.

Daily Meal Plan Example

Breakfast: Oatmeal with berries and milk

Snack: Apple with almond butter

Lunch: Grilled chicken, brown rice, steamed broccoli

Snack: Yogurt with granola

Dinner: Salmon, sweet potato, mixed vegetables
