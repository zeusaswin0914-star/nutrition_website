👶 Toddler Diet Plan (Ages 1-3)

Updated: December 2025

Daily Calorie Needs: 1000-1400 calories per day

Key Nutrition Focus

  • Whole milk: 16-24 oz daily (fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K)
  • Proteins: 13-19g daily (eggs, chicken, soft meats, beans)
  • Iron: 7-11mg daily (fortified cereals, meat, beans)
  • Calcium: 700mg daily (milk, cheese, yogurt)

Sample Daily Meal Plan

Breakfast (8 AM)

  • Oatmeal (1/4 cup cooked)
  • 1 egg, scrambled
  • Whole milk (8 oz)
  • Soft banana pieces

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM)

  • Soft cheese cubes
  • Apple slices (soft)
  • Water

Lunch (12 PM)

  • Soft chicken (2 oz)
  • Sweet potato (1/4 cup)
  • Peas (soft, 1/4 cup)
  • Whole milk (8 oz)

Afternoon Snack (3 PM)

  • Yogurt (4 oz)
  • Soft fruit (peach, prune)
  • Water

Dinner (6 PM)

  • Ground beef (2 oz, soft)
  • Rice (1/4 cup cooked)
  • Carrots (soft, 1/4 cup)
  • Whole milk (8 oz)

Safety Tips

  • Avoid choking hazards (nuts, popcorn, hard candies)
  • Cut food into small, soft pieces
  • Introduce one new food at a time
  • Always supervise mealtimes
{% elif age_group == 'child' %}

🧒 Child Diet Plan (Ages 4-8)

Updated: December 2025

Daily Calorie Needs: 1200-1600 calories per day (varies by activity)

Key Nutrition Focus

  • Proteins: 19-25g daily (lean meats, fish, eggs, beans, nuts)
  • Calcium: 1000mg daily (low-fat milk, yogurt, cheese)
  • Iron: 8-10mg daily (fortified cereals, red meat, spinach)
  • Whole Grains: At least half daily grains from whole sources

Sample Daily Meal Plan

Breakfast (7 AM)

  • Whole grain cereal (1 cup)
  • Low-fat milk (1 cup)
  • Berries (1/2 cup)
  • Toast with 1 tbsp peanut butter

Mid-Morning Snack (9:30 AM)

  • Apple with almond butter
  • Water or low-fat milk

Lunch (12 PM)

  • Grilled chicken breast (3 oz)
  • Brown rice (1/2 cup)
  • Steamed broccoli (1/2 cup)
  • Low-fat yogurt (1 cup)

Afternoon Snack (3 PM)

  • Cheese cubes (1 oz)
  • Whole grain crackers (5)
  • Water

Dinner (6:30 PM)

  • Salmon (3 oz)
  • Sweet potato (1 medium)
  • Mixed vegetables (1 cup)
  • Low-fat milk (1 cup)

Tips for Picky Eaters

  • Involve them in meal planning and cooking
  • Make food fun with colorful plates
  • Try new foods alongside familiar favorites
  • Keep portion sizes reasonable
  • Limit snacks close to meals
{% elif age_group == 'teen' %}

👦 Teen Diet Plan (Ages 9-18)

Updated: December 2025

Daily Calorie Needs: 1600-2400 calories per day (varies by sex and activity)

Key Nutrition Focus

  • Proteins: 34-52g daily (supports growth and muscle development)
  • Calcium: 1300mg daily (bone density building — critical for teens)
  • Iron: 8-15mg daily (especially important for menstruating girls: 15mg)
  • Physical Activity: 60+ minutes daily recommended

Sample Daily Meal Plan

Breakfast (7 AM)

  • Eggs (2), scrambled
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices)
  • Orange juice (8 oz)
  • Avocado slices

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM)

  • Greek yogurt (6 oz)
  • Granola (1/4 cup)
  • Berries

Lunch (1 PM)

  • Turkey sandwich (3 oz meat, whole wheat bread)
  • Lettuce, tomato, cheese
  • Carrot sticks with hummus
  • Low-fat milk (1 cup)

Pre-Exercise Snack (4 PM)

  • Banana with 1 tbsp almond butter
  • Water

Dinner (7 PM)

  • Lean beef (4 oz)
  • Pasta with whole wheat (1.5 cups)
  • Spinach salad with olive oil dressing
  • Low-fat milk (1 cup)

Special Considerations

  • Iron: Girls need 15mg daily (especially if menstruating). Include red meat, poultry, fortified cereals.
  • Calcium: Peak bone building years — ensure 1300mg daily from dairy or fortified alternatives.
  • Hydration: Drink water before, during, and after sports/exercise (not sugary drinks).
  • Healthy Habits: Avoid extreme dieting and energy drinks.
{% elif age_group == 'adult' %}

👨 Adult Diet Plan (Ages 19-65)

Updated: December 2025

Daily Calorie Needs: 1800-2500 calories per day (varies by sex, age, and activity level)

Key Nutrition Focus

  • Proteins: 46-56g daily (supports muscle maintenance and repair)
  • Fiber: 25-35g daily (from whole grains, fruits, vegetables)
  • Healthy Fats: 20-35% of calories from unsaturated fats (olive oil, nuts, fish)
  • Calcium: 1000-1200mg daily (bone health, especially for women)

Sample Daily Meal Plan

Breakfast (7 AM)

  • Oatmeal (1 cup cooked)
  • Berries (1 cup)
  • Almonds (1/4 cup)
  • Green tea

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM)

  • Greek yogurt (6 oz)
  • Honey drizzle
  • Walnuts

Lunch (12:30 PM)

  • Grilled salmon (5 oz)
  • Quinoa (1/2 cup)
  • Roasted vegetables (1.5 cups)
  • Olive oil dressing

Afternoon Snack (3 PM)

  • Apple slices
  • Almond butter (1 tbsp)
  • Water

Dinner (7 PM)

  • Lean chicken (5 oz)
  • Sweet potato (1 medium)
  • Broccoli (1.5 cups)
  • Low-fat milk (1 cup)

Health Tips

  • Cardiovascular Health: Limit sodium and include omega-3 fatty acids (fish, flaxseed).
  • Bone Health: Weight-bearing exercise + calcium-rich foods.
  • Weight Management: Balanced meals with lean proteins and whole grains.
  • Moderation: Control portion sizes, limit added sugars and alcohol.
{% elif age_group == 'senior' %}

👴 Senior Diet Plan (Ages 65+)

Updated: December 2025

Daily Calorie Needs: 1600-2400 calories per day (lower than younger adults due to reduced activity)

Key Nutrition Focus

  • Proteins: 1.0-1.2 g/kg body weight (supports muscle maintenance and bone strength)
  • Calcium & Vitamin D: Prevent osteoporosis (1200mg calcium, 800-1000 IU vitamin D)
  • B12: From fortified foods or supplements (absorption decreases with age)
  • Fiber: 25-30g daily (manage digestion and heart health)

Sample Daily Meal Plan

Breakfast (8 AM)

  • Fortified oatmeal (1 cup)
  • Fortified milk (1 cup)
  • Soft fruit (banana, blueberries)
  • Honey

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM)

  • Plain yogurt (6 oz, fortified)
  • Soft granola (2 tbsp)
  • Water

Lunch (1 PM)

  • Soft-cooked chicken (4 oz)
  • Mashed sweet potato (1/2 cup)
  • Soft-cooked spinach (1/2 cup)
  • Fortified milk (1 cup)

Afternoon Snack (3 PM)

  • String cheese (1 stick)
  • Soft apple slices
  • Water

Dinner (6 PM)

  • Salmon (4 oz, omega-3 rich)
  • Brown rice (1/2 cup)
  • Soft-cooked carrots (1/2 cup)
  • Fortified milk (1 cup)

Special Health Considerations

  • Chewing Difficulty: Soft, easy-to-chew foods (soups, mashed vegetables, tender meats).
  • Digestion: Adequate fiber and hydration; smaller, frequent meals.
  • Medication Interactions: Check with doctor about food-drug interactions.
  • Cognitive Health: Include antioxidant-rich foods (blueberries, spinach, walnuts).
  • Blood Pressure: Limit salt; include potassium-rich foods (bananas, beans, potatoes).
